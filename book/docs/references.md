# References

This section provides a comprehensive list of all primary sources and key references cited throughout the "Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics" book. All entries adhere to the APA 7th edition citation style. This list is crucial for academic rigor, acknowledging the work of others, and enabling readers to explore topics in greater depth.

## R.1 Understanding APA Citation Style

The American Psychological Association (APA) style is widely used in technical and scientific fields for its clarity and consistency. Key elements include:
*   **Author-Date System**: In-text citations include the author's last name and the year of publication (e.g., (Smith, 2020)).
*   **Reference List**: A complete alphabetical list of all sources cited in the text, providing full publication details.
*   **DOI (Digital Object Identifier)**: Essential for journal articles and often for other online sources.

## R.2 Compiling and Formatting References

The process of creating a robust reference list involves:
1.  **Collecting Sources**: As you write, keep a meticulous record of all sources consulted.
2.  **Verifying Information**: Ensure all bibliographic details (authors, dates, titles, publishers, URLs/DOIs) are accurate and complete.
3.  **Formatting**: Apply APA 7th edition rules for each source type (journal article, book, website, software documentation, etc.).

## R.3 Maintaining Consistency and Accuracy

*   **Reference Management Software**: Tools like Zotero, Mendeley, or EndNote can automate much of the citation and bibliography generation process, ensuring consistency.
*   **Regular Review**: Periodically review your reference list and in-text citations for accuracy and completeness.
*   **Link Validation**: For online sources, periodically check that all URLs are still active and point to the correct content.
